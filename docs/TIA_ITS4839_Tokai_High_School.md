## 1. Purpose of Study

This report summarises an assessment of the transport impacts expected as part of the proposed Tokai High School in Bergvliet, Cape Town. This school development forms part of the Department of Education's Rapid Schools Implementation Program.

This assessment aims to identify potential constraints within the surrounding transport network, recommend appropriate mitigation measures, and ensure that applicable operations are maintained.

---

## 2. Locality

This school will be developed on Erf 1061 in Bergvliet, Cape Town. This school will be located in the southwestern quadrant of the Ruskin Road/Aristea Road intersection. See Figure A1.1 and Figure A1.2 for the wider view and zoomed-in view Locality Plans.

## 9. Existing Traffic Operations

The Existing Traffic conditions are based on existing intersection configurations and controls, and the traffic volumes counted on Tuesday, 10 June 2025. Traffic counts were conducted at six study intersections on a typical weekday, during the morning (06:30 to 09:00), midday (13:00 to 15:30), and evening (15:30 to 18:00) peak periods. The peak hours within these periods were identified and used in the capacity analysis. Capacity analysis was undertaken using **Traffix** and **Sidra Intersection 9.0** software.

Based on the capacity analysis, most study intersections currently operate acceptably (i.e. Level of Service LOS A & B). Although the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection currently operates at an overall acceptable level of service, the right turn movement along the northern and western approaches operates at **LOS E / LOS F** during the AM and PM peak hours. However, future upgrades along Main Road, as discussed in Section 5, are anticipated to significantly improve the operational performance of this intersection.

Refer to Figures A4, A5, and A6 in Annexure A for a summary of the Existing Traffic peak hour volumes and operations at the study intersections.

## 10. Background Traffic Operations

The 2030 Background Traffic volumes were calculated by escalating the existing counted traffic volumes with a **1.5% growth rate per year over a five-year period** at all intersections. The traffic growth rate is in accordance with the South African Trip Data Manual (COTO TMH17, 2013) and represents the low growth potential of the surrounding residential areas near the proposed Tokai High School.

Historical data from 2014 were obtained at an intersection adjacent to the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection, which indicates a growth rate ranging between 1% and 2%. The 1.5% growth rate is therefore considered a realistic estimate of the horizon year's traffic volumes. This conservative growth assumption is further supported by the fact that the area is largely built up with limited vacant land available for new development. The established nature of the surrounding neighbourhoods and the absence of planned large-scale developments reinforce the expectation of minimal traffic growth over the analysis period.

Based on this capacity analysis, most study intersections will continue to operate at acceptable levels of service, delays and volume-capacity ratios. As noted in Section 9, the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection is expected to continue to experience operational constraints due to existing limitations in intersection geometry and control. During this scenario, all peak hours experience **LOS E/F** for the right turn movement along the northern and western approaches at the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection. The upgrades discussed in Section 5 are expected to redirect traffic and reduce the capacity constraints and delays evident at the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection.

See Figures A7, A8, and A9 in Annexure A for a summary of the Background Traffic peak hour volumes and traffic operations at the study intersections.

## 11. Site Access

**Existing Access:** There is currently no existing access to Erf 1061.

**Future Access:** The proposed development includes a dedicated one-way internal circulation system within the school grounds, with an ingress from Ruskin Road and an egress onto Aristea Road.

- **Ingress:** The proposed entrance is located on Ruskin Road, forming a third leg to the existing Ruskin Road/Leyden Road intersection. This location is approximately **275 metres west** of the Ruskin Road/Aristea Road intersection.

- **Egress:** The exit is proposed at the existing Aristea Road cul-de-sac, approximately **150 metres south** of the Ruskin Road/Aristea Road intersection.

This configuration provides several key advantages:

- **Minimises Traffic Disruption:** The one-way access system will reduce turning movement conflicts at both access points, which are located at existing, low-volume intersections.
- **Reduces Pressure on Surrounding Roads:** By allowing vehicles to circulate within the school property — including drop-off and pick-up activities — parents and staff will not need to queue along adjacent roads.
- **Improves Safety:** The separate entry and exit points reduce the potential for conflicting vehicle movements on- and off-site, improving both vehicular and pedestrian safety during peak school times.
- **Alignment with Existing Road Infrastructure:** The proposed access points are situated at existing and accepted intersections, eliminating the need for new access breaks along residential frontages.

It is proposed that the ingress and egress be constructed as **raised intersections** to further enforce slower speeds along these roads.

Refer to the Site Plan and Figure A11 for the proposed location of the access in Annexure A.

## 14. Total Traffic Operations

The 2030 Total Traffic volumes were calculated by adding the expected trips from the proposed school development to the Background Traffic volumes.

Based on the Total Traffic capacity analysis, the study intersections would continue to operate acceptably at **LOS A, B, C & D**. The operations at the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection will continue to deteriorate, with the LOS during the AM peak hour worsening to **LOS F**. The Midday and PM peak hours still operate at overall acceptable levels of service; however, the volume-to-capacity ratio is **greater than 1.0**.

No upgrades are required at the study intersections except for the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection. It is however uncertain what the timeframe of implementation of the proposed upgrades noted in Section 5 are.

Operations during all peak hours will continue to operate acceptably in the immediate surrounding area of the proposed school. Hence, no road upgrades are required or proposed at any of the study intersections as part of the Total Traffic scenario from a capacity analysis point of view.

It is however proposed to construct a **mini roundabout at the Ruskin Road / Aristea Road intersection** in an effort to further minimise the traffic impact on the road network by improving the flow at this intersection.

See Figures A15, A16, and A17 in Annexure A for the Total Traffic peak hour volumes and operations at the study intersections.

## 17. Parking

The site is located within a standard parking area as defined by the Municipal Planning Amendment By-Law (CoCT, 2019).

The required parking provision applicable for the proposed school in a standard area includes:

- A parking rate of **1 bay per classroom/office**
- Plus a stop-and-drop facility at a rate of **1 bay per 20 learners**

Based on this, **95 bays** are required for the school. This should include 2 bays for people with physical disabilities.

The SDP indicates that a total of **120 parking bays** will be provided — **98 parking bays** will be provided on the school site, while **22 bays** are provided on-street, along Ruskin Road. Sufficient parking will therefore be provided.

## 18. Conclusion & Recommendations

The report summarises an investigation of the transport impacts expected as part of the proposed Tokai High School, planned on Erf 1061, in Bergvliet, Cape Town.

### Summary of Findings

- **Land Use/Extent** – The proposed development comprises a public high school accommodating 1 120 learners, with associated classrooms, offices, and facilities.

- **2025 Existing Traffic Conditions** – Most study intersections currently operate at acceptable levels of service. However, the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection experiences operational constraints during the AM and PM peak hours, with right-turn movements operating at LOS E/F.

- **2030 Background Traffic Conditions** – Under background traffic growth (1.5% annually), most intersections continue to function acceptably. The Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection, however, remains a constraint, with critical movements deteriorating to LOS E/F during all peak periods.

- **Trip Generation** – The proposed high school is expected to generate approximately **840, 392, and 280** additional vehicle trips during the weekday AM, midday, and PM peak hours, respectively.

- **2030 Total Traffic Conditions** – Most study intersections will continue to operate within acceptable limits under the total traffic scenario. The Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection is expected to further deteriorate during the AM peak, with LOS F and volume-to-capacity ratios exceeding 1.0. All other study intersections are projected to operate acceptably without the need for upgrades. A mini roundabout is proposed at the Ruskin Road/Aristea Road intersection to further enhance traffic circulation and minimise queuing and turning conflicts during peak drop-off and pick-up periods.

- **Mitigation through Planned Upgrades** – The City of Cape Town's PROW 2023 outlines proposed upgrades to Main Road, including geometric changes and signalisation of the Zwaans Road/Glen Alpine Way intersection. These improvements are anticipated to significantly reduce the pressure on the Main Road/Dreyersdal Road intersection by redistributing traffic and improving flow. However, the timing of these upgrades remains uncertain. It is therefore recommended that coordination with the relevant authorities continue to ensure alignment of school opening timelines with the delivery of road infrastructure.

- **Site Access** – A one-way internal circulation route is proposed, with ingress from Ruskin Road and egress onto Aristea Road. This design is expected to reduce congestion on surrounding streets and improve safety by accommodating pick-up and drop-off activities on-site.

- **Public Transport** – The existing public transport services in the area should be sufficient. Hence, no additional facilities are recommended.

- **Pedestrians and Cyclists** – The following upgrades are recommended:
  - Surfaced 2 m wide sidewalks along the verge of Ruskin Road and Aristea Road on the side of the school.
  - Two raised pedestrian crossings proposed south of the vehicular entrance ingress.

- **Parking provision** – A total of 120 parking bays will be provided (98 on-site and 22 on-street), exceeding the minimum requirement of 95 bays, including two bays for people with disabilities.

### Recommendations

From a traffic engineering perspective, the proposed Tokai High School development can be supported, subject to the following recommendations:

**1. Access Design Implementation:**
- Construct the proposed ingress (from Ruskin Road) and egress (onto Aristea Road) as raised intersections.

**2. Intersection Improvement:**
- Construct a mini roundabout at the Ruskin Road/Aristea Road intersection to manage school-related peak hour traffic and improve turning movements.

**3. Non-Motorised Transport Enhancements:**
- Construct 2 m wide surfaced sidewalks along the school frontage on Ruskin and Aristea Roads.

> On condition that the above measures are implemented, it is recommended that the proposed development be approved from a transport engineering point of view.
