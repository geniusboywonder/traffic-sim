To compare your model's output with the professional simulations, you should use the following files, which contain the same categories of data (events and
  road-level metrics) found in your CSVs:

  1. For SUMO Comparison
  SUMO provides the most detailed microscopic data. You should look in sim/sumo/:

   * Vehicle Performance: tripinfo-L.xml
       * Equivalent to your traffic-sim-log: This contains the total travel time, waiting time, and "time loss" (delay) for every single vehicle. It is the best
         file for comparing average trip durations and total system delay.
   * Road/Edge Statistics: summary-L.xml (if generated) or fcd-L.xml
       * Equivalent to your traffic-road-stats: FCD (Floating Car Data) tracks vehicle speeds on specific "edges" (roads). You can use this to see if SUMO
         predicts the same "stopped" vs "slowing" vehicle counts on Christopher or Ruskin Rd that your model does.

  2. For UXsim Comparison
  UXsim is used for broader network validation. Its outputs are often processed directly into JSON for the frontend:

   * Network Flow Metrics: public/sim-results/scenario-L.json
       * This file is the "final result" displayed when you click the Results button in the app. It contains the aggregated speed and density data that UXsim
         calculated for the entire network.
   * Raw Outputs: Check the sim/ folder for any .csv or .pkl files generated by uxsim_runner.py. UXsim typically outputs time-series data of "Link" (road)
     performance.

  Key Metrics to Compare:
   1. Mean Travel Time: Does a car from Firgrove Way take significantly longer in Tokai-Sim vs SUMO?
   2. Saturation Timeline: At what sim-time does Ruskin Rd hit "Stopped" status in your model vs SUMO? (Look for the v < 0.5 threshold in your stats CSV).
   3. Rat-Run Activation: Does your model's 6-10% congestion trigger match the moment SUMO starts diverting traffic to residential side-streets?

  Recommendation: Start with sim/sumo/tripinfo-L.xml. It is the most "human-readable" summary of how well the simulation performed compared to your log.
